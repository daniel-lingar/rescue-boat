# The 26 Laws of Survival – Overview
*One‑sentence summaries from the WRH Master Curriculum*

1. Your alarm never shuts off.
2. You're carrying ghosts you never met.
3. A dangerous shelter is still a shelter.
4. What you couldn't mourn haunts you.
5. Work becomes the war you can win.
6. You were looking in a broken mirror.
7. You learned to disappear.
8. You scan every room for exits.
9. You stop when you can't fight or run.
10. You leave your body.
11. The system that should help hurts again.
12. Parts of you want different things.
13. They called it noncompliance.
14. Your gas pedal sticks.
15. Shame is the glue.
16. You don't know where you end.
17. Exhaustion is a warning light.
18. Anger is grief with teeth.
19. Crisis is a turning point.
20. Seeing the machine changes it.
21. You don't have to fix it, just feel it.
22. Recovery isn't a straight line.
23. Your nervous system needs others.
24. You don't just survive – you grow.
25. You get to write the next chapter.
26. Staying stoic kept you alive – now it's a cage.

*These laws are heuristic tools for pattern recognition, not clinical diagnostics. For clinical application, consult the full WRH curriculum or a trauma‑informed provider.*
