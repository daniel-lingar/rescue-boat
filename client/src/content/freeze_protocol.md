# Freeze Response Protocol for Courtrooms
*(For judges, probation officers, defense attorneys, and court staff)*

**The Problem:** A defendant misses a hearing. The court assumes defiance. In fact, the person may have experienced a freeze response.

**The Science:** Under perceived threat, the brainstem overrides conscious control. The person is not choosing to disobey; their nervous system is protecting them by shutting down voluntary movement.

**What works (instead of punishment):**

| Instead of… | Try… |
|-------------|------|
| Issuing a warrant immediately | Brief continuance with a calm, clear explanation |
| Raising your voice | Low, steady, predictable tone |
| Interpreting silence as disrespect | “Can you hear me? Nod if yes.” |
| Threatening jail for failure to appear | Written reminder card with date, time, and check‑in contact |

**Documentation note:** *“Defendant exhibited physiological freeze response; accommodated with brief recess.”* Protects staff legally while reinforcing the protocol.
